Racial Disparities in Books and Publishing Industry

By Milette Millington

March 15, 2021

Some of the best books in 2020, which highlight racial disparities in the books and publishing industry regarding employment. (Photo Credit: Peter Ardito, People Magazine)

Hidden Valley Road: Inside the Mind of an American Family

This book is about a family with 12 children, six of which have been diagnosed with schizophrenia.

Hamnet

This book is a fictional account of the life of William Shakespeare's son Hamnet, who died in 1596 at 11 years old.

A Promised Land

This is Barack Obama's memoir, in which he reflects on his time in the White House as the 44th U.S. President.

What Are You Going Through

This novel explores human connection and the changing nature of our relationships in life.

Notes on a Silencing

This is Lacy Crawford's memoir, in which she takes readers on a journey of how a young woman discovers her self-confidence after a traumatic experience in her life.

Caste: The Origins of Our Discontents

This book, written by African-American female journalist Isabel Wilkerson, discusses the history of the way African-Americans have been and continue to be treated in this country.

Writers and Lovers

This is Lily King's novel that deals with the challenges of being an aspiring writer.

Monogamy: A Novel

Sue Miller's book demonstrates how much truth depends on what people are shown over time.

The Vanishing Half

Brit Bennett's book showcases how much a person's past can shape who the person is in their future.

The Lying Life of Adults

Elena Ferrante's book depicts the transitions in life from childhood to adolescence to adulthood of people in her hometown of Naples, Italy.
